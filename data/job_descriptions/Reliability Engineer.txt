Job Summary
A Reliability Engineer is responsible for improving equipment reliability, reducing failures,
and optimizing maintenance strategies using data analysis and engineering techniques. The
role focuses on maximizing uptime and minimizing operational risks.
Key Responsibilities

• Analyze equipment performance and identify failure patterns
• Conduct root cause failure analysis (RCFA) and implement corrective actions
• Develop and optimize preventive and predictive maintenance strategies
• Monitor reliability KPIs such as MTBF (Mean Time Between Failures) and MTTR
(Mean Time To Repair)
• Implement condition monitoring techniques (vibration analysis, thermography, oil
analysis)
• Collaborate with maintenance, production, and engineering teams
• Recommend design or process improvements for reliability enhancement
• Maintain reliability data, reports, and documentation
Required Skills
• Strong knowledge of reliability engineering principles
• Experience with maintenance strategies (preventive, predictive, RCM)
• Data analysis and problem-solving skills
• Familiarity with condition monitoring tools
• Knowledge of industrial equipment and systems
Qualifications
• B.E./B.Tech in Mechanical Engineering
• 3–7 years of experience
