Job Summary
A Power Plant Engineer is responsible for supporting the operation, monitoring, and
maintenance of power generation systems such as boilers, turbines, generators, and auxiliary
equipment. The role ensures safe, efficient, and continuous power production.
Key Responsibilities
• Monitor and operate power plant equipment (boilers, turbines, generators)
• Assist in maintaining optimal plant performance and efficiency
• Conduct routine inspections and log operational data
• Support preventive and corrective maintenance activities
• Identify operational issues and assist in troubleshooting
• Ensure compliance with safety, environmental, and regulatory standards
• Coordinate with maintenance, electrical, and instrumentation teams
• Maintain operational reports and documentation
• Follow standard operating procedures (SOPs) for plant operations

Required Skills
• Basic knowledge of thermal power systems and plant operations
• Understanding of boilers, turbines, and heat transfer principles
• Awareness of safety and environmental regulations
• Problem-solving and analytical skills
• Ability to work in shifts and industrial environments
Qualifications
• B.E./B.Tech in Mechanical Engineering
• 0–2 years of experience
