Job Summary
A Scientist in defense or space organizations works on advanced national projects such as
missiles, satellites, aircraft, and space missions, contributing to strategic and technological
development.
Key Responsibilities
• Design and develop advanced mechanical systems (aerospace, defense, robotics)
• Conduct research and testing for mission-critical systems
• Work on simulation, modeling, and prototype development
• Ensure compliance with national and international standards
• Collaborate with multidisciplinary teams
• Participate in field trials, launches, and system validation
• Document research findings and technical reports
Required Skills
• Strong fundamentals in mechanical/aerospace engineering
• Problem-solving and innovation skills
• Knowledge of advanced tools (CFD, FEA, simulation)
• High attention to detail and precision
Qualifications
• B.E./B.Tech or M.Tech in Mechanical/Aerospace Engineering
• Selection through exams like GATE, recruitment tests, or interviews
